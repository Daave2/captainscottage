Property Overview

Situated at 35 Longwestgate, Scarborough, North Yorkshire, YO11 1QB, Captain’s Cottage offers guests a unique blend of heritage and contemporary comfort. This historic mid-terrace property has been lovingly refurbished by new owners in mid-2024. Its rich maritime past is reflected in every detail—from period features to modern upgrades.

  • Heritage & Refurbishment: 19th-century cottage revitalized with updated decor, meticulous maintenance and enhanced guest comforts.
  • Layout: Two well-appointed bedrooms—first-floor double and light-filled second-floor twin—sleeping up to four.
  • Living & Kitchen: Cozy sitting room (electric fireplace, Smart TV, Wi-Fi) plus fully equipped kitchen/diner.
  • Outdoor Space: Decking & garden for alfresco dining; complimentary parking permit included.

Experience authentic Scarborough living just steps from the beach and Scarborough Castle.

Our Story

Captain’s Cottage has a rich maritime heritage rooted in Scarborough’s vibrant history. Built in the 19th century, the cottage witnessed the golden era of seaside resorts. In mid-2024, passionate new owners breathed fresh life into the property, preserving its historic character while modernizing amenities.

Today, Captain’s Cottage marries classic elegance with contemporary comfort—delivering history, luxury and genuine local hospitality.

What’s On?

Explore Scarborough

  • Scarborough Castle (~300 m) – 12th-century fortress crowning the headland.
  • Market Hall & Vaults (~550 m) – Victorian market hall, Mon–Sat 8am–5pm.
  • MV Regal Lady (~550 m) – 1930 steamship, National Historic Ship; Dunkirk veteran.
  • Scarborough Beach (~650 m) – Sandy beach & promenade.
  • St Martin-on-the-Hill (~650 m) – Bodley church with Pre-Raphaelite glass.
  • Scarborough Art Gallery (~650 m) – Grade II* Italianate villa; local art & heritage.
  • St Mary’s & Anne Brontë’s Grave (~700 m) – 12th-century parish church.
  • Peasholm Park (~850 m) – Oriental-themed 14-hectare park (1912).
  • Train Station (~900 m) – Links to York, Leeds and beyond.
